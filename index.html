Introduction

I worked on this project in my second year at Fanshawe. We were tasked to rebrand a existing local restaurant of our choice. The new rebrand would include a new logo, business card, envelope designs, T-shirt designs, Store Signage, menu board & Food Truck.

I choose to pick Tony’s Pizza, as it is one of the oldest places in London, ON. The existing brand design looked outdated and old. I wanted to target the younger population with the new rebrand, and increase their online presence.

About Tony’s Pizza
& Original Design

Tony’s Pizza was established in 1961 by Tony Lanni. He was an immigrant and it was his dream to open his own Italian restaurant. Tony’s Pizza has been running from almost 60 years has a total of 3 restaurants in London, ON.

Since 1961, there has not been any change in the overall design, that is why it the design style looks outdated. With the new rebranding I focused on giving Tony’s Pizza a modern look but still preserving the history associated with the brand.

Redesigning the Logo

Redesigning Tony’s logo was a challenge. The existing logo has never been changed since 1961, thus it had a 60s look. The original logo is hand-written script style logo in red and white. My initial thought was to preserve the same style, so I started with sketching in a similar script style.

In my sketches you can see I was trying different styles, shapes and formations for the new logo. I didn’t know what I was going to select later, but I had a good feeling about the sketches, so I moved further with some selections.
